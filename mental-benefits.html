Mental Health Benefits

Mental Benefits

See below to make sure this is something for you

  • Relieve Stress

    Feeling stressed is normal for a while. Stress has always accompanied humans. It allows us to mobilize our body to achieve its goals. Over time, we can see that a people are exposed to excessive stress. Correctly regulating the level of stress and proper rest are crucial for maintaining balance and emotional peace. Ice swimming allows you to reset the human body. The moment of entering the water is cleansing. People stop thinking about their problems and focus only on the present sensations. This behaviour allows you to relax. Regular use of such rest will enable to restore the body's proper functioning and takes care of wellbeing.

  • Burnout recovery

    The best example of dealing with burnout recovery is Wim Hof, who is known for his incredible achievements. He has over 20 Guinness World Records on his account. In his case, exposure to cold has significant positive effects on the body and mind. Scientists have found evidence that practitioners of the Wim Hof Method are able to influence his nervous system voluntarily. Each person reacts differently to the cold, but you will find that most beginner ice swimmers experience tremendous wellbeing changes. It is worth trying this kind of therapy.

  • Improve cold tolerance

    A body that is subjected to new sensations reacts better and more gently to them over time. By regularly ice swimming, we can see a greater tolerance to cold. To some extent, this is the desired effect. The human body and the brain feel better if it functions in a friendly environment, which is a comfortable temperature.

  • Deal with depression

    Depression is a very complex disorder. It is hard to say clearly only the benefits of ice swimming in case of depression treatment. Many scientists agree that this type of treatment should take place on many levels. Taking medication may be necessary, but certainly, a person's feelings and attitudes play a huge role. New experiences of changing the environment and swimming in a group can play a significant role in treating depression. Any exercise is good for the body. Open water swimming in a group of friends or other community helps create achievements and make sense of life. Positive experiences from such activity make it easier to cope with problems and give a chance for treatment.

  • Boost concentration

    When practice wild swimming, you can be focused only on your body and nature. The other sensations and thoughts go by the wayside, and they don't matter at the moment. After each swimming session, our brain is devoid of unnecessary problems. We can take a fresh look at the world around us. Exposing the body to cold can increase the concentration level.

  • Increase happiness

    General knowledge say ice swimming can regulate dopamine transmissions. This is because if we voluntarily do exercise such as swimming, our body recognizes it as a good effect. By repeating activities that are pleasant for the body, we make it easier for the body to regularly release the dopamine it needs. Cortisol release can be increased in short term. Cortisol increased from 99 ng/ml to 133 ng/ml in the untrained, and from 101 ng/ml to 137 ng/ml (p < 0.05) in the cold-trained persons within 30 minutes after cold water immersion, and decreased thereafter (p < 0.01)

  • Accelerates release of endorphins

    Research shows that physical activity improves the secretion of endorphins. What happens to the human body while immersed in water is difficult to describe cold water swimming has a leg up on the traditional gym or run endorphin rush, since you're not only working your muscles, but shocking your body as soon as you're exposed to the low-temperature water. The intense release of pleasure from endorphins and increased energy following a cool dip, will leave you revitalized and ready to take on the world.
