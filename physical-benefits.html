Mental Benefits

  • Reduce blood pressure

    Several studies have described a positive effect on the cardiovascular system and cardiovascular risk factors. Research shows that people who use cold water swimming may notice lowered blood pressure. Of course, you should check with your local GP or cardiologist before starting your ice swimming. There are several cardiovascular contraindications, and for the sake of health, you should consult your doctor. One of the ways to help lower blood pressure is to take care of lifestyle. Cold water swimming can be a tiny part of the whole lifestyle.

  • Increase your libido

    Human libido is a very complex concept. To maintain balance, we must take care of the health of the entire body. In addition to physicality, the emotional state and hormones in the body are also highly important. Earlier, I gave examples of how our body can be supported by regularly practising ice swimming. A positive attitude and good health have a positive effect on libido. Satisfaction with life can result in greater self-confidence, which directly affects the quality of life. Research shows that cold water can increase oestrogen and testosterone production, which has an impact on libido.

  • Helps in the weight loss process – improve metabolism

    Being in cold water, we force our hearts to work more intensively. This is due to the need to maintain body temperature at a natural level. A cold environment increases the demand for calories that must be used to compensate for the energy deficit. The above statement does not show that much more significant amounts of food can be eaten when practising ice swimming, but this therapy may help reduce body weight. Additionally, cold water improves the circulatory system's functioning and makes the skin firmer and more elastic.

  • Boost your immune system

    Going into cold water forces the body to adapt to this environment. One of the ways of adapting is increased production a white cells to prevent infection. Staying in the water frequently and practising such activity significantly improves the immune system. However, it cannot be forgotten that it only works if our body is fully healthy. You cannot weaken the immune system, which is already fighting the infection.

  • Workout recovery

    By practising cold water swimming, we can notice that our body regenerates faster and feels less the effects of training and temporary can offer pain relief from sore muscles, which is the desired effect. Not everyone can feel such benefits, but it is worth remembering that it can be an added advantage for active people.
    It is worth remembering that to better sleep by relieving stress and giving you control over your emotions can affect your body and mind.

  • Migraine relief

    As previously mentioned, cold can affect the level of pain we feel. Cold is an anaesthetic and can be anti-inflammatory. Research shows that people who have undergone cold therapy and use such treatments regularly may experience less intense migraine recurrences compared to the control group. On the example of Beth Francis, who has completed a 100-day challenge of cold sea swimming, we can see a significant reduction in migraine headaches. In her case, the frequency decreased from about 25 times to 15.
